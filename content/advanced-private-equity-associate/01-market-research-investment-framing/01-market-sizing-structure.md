# Market Sizing and Structure

## Learning Objectives

- Evaluate market size and growth using both top-down and bottom-up methodologies
- Assess market structure and fragmentation to identify consolidation opportunities
- Distinguish between addressable market, serviceable market, and realistic penetration
- Identify data sources and methodologies to validate market assumptions

## Written Guide

### Understanding Market Context

- Why market sizing matters for investment decisions
- Total addressable market (TAM) vs. serviceable addressable market (SAM)
- Market growth as a tailwind or headwind for value creation

### Top-Down Market Sizing

- Industry reports and third-party research
- Macro indicators and adjacencies
- Limitations of top-down approaches

### Bottom-Up Market Sizing

- Customer cohort analysis
- Unit economics extrapolation
- Channel and geography-based builds
- When bottom-up is more credible than top-down

### Market Structure Analysis

- Fragmentation vs. consolidation dynamics
- Identifying the top 5-10 players by share
- Regional vs. national vs. global market structures
- Barriers to entry and competitive moats at the market level

### Data Sources and Validation

- Industry associations and trade publications
- Government data and regulatory filings
- Expert networks and primary research
- Triangulating estimates from multiple sources

### Translating Market Sizing to Investment Logic

- How market size affects valuation and returns potential
- Growth vs. mature markets: implications for thesis
- Market share gain vs. market growth as value drivers

## Key Takeaways

- Market sizing informs valuation, growth potential, and value creation strategy
- Use both top-down (industry reports, macro) and bottom-up (unit economics, cohorts) approaches
- Market structure (fragmented vs. consolidated) determines consolidation opportunity
- Validate estimates using multiple data sources; triangulate for credibility
- Distinguish between total market, serviceable market, and realistic market share penetration
